# TASTE_FRAMEWORK — Make Weekly Meal Prep Actually Taste Good

This is the practical flavor system to prevent "healthy but boring" plans.

## The 5-Point Flavor Check (every meal)
1. **Salt**: enough seasoning in the base
2. **Acid**: lemon/lime/vinegar at finish
3. **Heat**: chili flakes, chili crisp, or hot sauce (optional)
4. **Texture**: include one crunchy element
5. **Freshness**: herbs or fresh aromatics at the end

If a meal feels flat, usually it's missing acid + crunch.

## Weekly Sauce Strategy (2 sauces max)
Keep it simple: make two sauces on Sunday and use them across most meals.

### Sauce A: Lemon-Herb Yogurt
- Greek yogurt
- Lemon juice + zest
- Garlic
- Salt + black pepper
- Dill or parsley

Use on bowls, shrimp, eggs, salads.

### Sauce B: Chipotle-Lime
- Greek yogurt (or light mayo)
- Chipotle (powder or adobo)
- Lime juice
- Cumin
- Small honey pinch

Use on taco bowls, tofu, roasted vegetables.

## Protein Taste Upgrades (high impact)
- **Tofu**: press + sear hard first, then glaze (never steam-soft tofu in sauce)
- **Shrimp**: 10-minute marinade (lemon, garlic, paprika, salt) before skillet
- **Beans/Lentils**: toast spices in oil first; finish with acid
- **Egg whites**: cook with pre-sautéed vegetables + feta/chili flakes

## Texture Toolkit
Add one per meal:
- toasted seeds or nuts
- crispy chickpeas
- pickled onions
- shredded cabbage/slaw
- cucumber + herbs

## Practical Rule for Meal Prep Week
- Sunday base should be slightly under-sauced.
- Add sauce + fresh acid while reheating/serving.
- This keeps flavor bright all week and avoids soggy meals.

## Anti-Bland Rescue (60 seconds)
If today's container tastes dull:
1) pinch of salt
2) squeeze lemon/lime
3) spoon of sauce
4) crunchy topper

Usually enough to fix it without recooking.
